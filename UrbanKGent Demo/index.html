Abstract

Urban Knowledge Graph (UrbanKG) has recently worked as an emerging building block to distill critical knowledge from multi-sourced urban data for many urban application scenarios. Despite its promising benefits on urban downstream tasks, urban knowledge graph construction (UrbanKGC) still suffers heavy reliance on manual effort, which hinders its potential advancement. This paper presents UrbanKGent, a domain-oriented agent construction framework, to construct an UrbanKGC agent for automated urban knowledge graph construction. Specifically, we first construct the knowledgeable instruction set for UrbanKGC tasks (e.g., relational triplet extraction and knowledge graph completion.) via knowledge-aware chain-of-thought and decomposed multi-view prompting. Moreover, we propose derive tool-augmented trajectory interaction to enhance and refine generated trajectories for hybrid instruction fine-tuning. We perform experiments on two real-world urban datasets and the empirical results demonstrated that the proposed tuning framework can enable Llama 2-13B to significantly outperform stronger LLMs such as GPT-4 in UrbanKGC tasks.
